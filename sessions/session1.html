The Ballad of the Bell and the Bandits

Carried on cold winds and whispered in inns

The mid‑day bell rang sharp and clear,
A sound that stirred both doubt and cheer.
And Mallyn passed with grin so wide,
Off chasing dreams the world would hide.
The silent bell, a warning slight,
Brought creeping fear to smother night.
But boots were eyed and gold was sought,
By Prall and rogues with hunger hot.
“Your coin and boots!” their leader cried,
Then charged as fury filled his stride.
A battleaxe sang through the glade,
A thundered blow the forest made.
Halberds flashed bright, the bandits fell,
Their shouts cut short by steel and yell.
The silent bell, a warning slight,
Brought creeping fear to smother night.
To Northkeep’s walls the party came,
Where Umbrusk spoke with guarded frame:
“A patrol is lost, and Tomas too-
Find where they went, and what they knew.”
The church now held the weak and worn,
Where healing failed and hope was torn.
A bard brought song to soothe the soul,
A crude bell carved by trembling hands.
The silent bell, a warning slight,
Brought creeping fear to smother night.
Soup rose and swirled in tavern air,
As questions stirred the patrons there.
The barkeep frowned, the barmaid sighed,
And secrets shifted, half‑denied.
The cellars bare, yet still they sought -
For Tomas crept where he was caught.
The soup was thin, the stores were low,
And Northkeep starved beneath the snow.
A mishap in the dead of night
Set magic screaming in its fright.
An alarm spell tripped, the inn awoke -
A startled crowd, confusion’s cloak.
The rogue alone saw truth unspelled -
No bells had tolled since autumn fell.
A fisherman confessed his fear,
A husband’s guilt weighed heavy here.
The silent bell, a warning slight,
Brought creeping fear to smother night.
And now the trail through woods grows cold -
The patrol awaits, their fate untold.
